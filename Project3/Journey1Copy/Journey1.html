GERTONTOLOGY SUIT

FLAGG HALL TO SCHOOL OF ART + DESIGN

The first exercise featured a simulation of common impairments experienced by members of the aging population.

I embodied these impairments by putting on a gerentology suit, also known as a GERT suit, which is an amalgamation of different devices that simulate many of the common disabilities experienced by the elderly.

The first item of the suit were a pair of gloves to simulate the loss of grip strength and reduced tactile perception.

At this moment in the process of putting on the suit, the discomfort had not quite set in yet.

The next item of the suit was a belt wrapped around my abdomen to restrict mobility and the bending of my spine.

With the constriction of my abdomen and the added inabilty to bend my spine, my anxiety was stimulated and began to rise steadily.

(At this point, the distinction between fear and anxiety must be made. I did not feel a sense of danger. Instead, I was consciously allowing myself to be immersed in the experience, letting my body freely and naturally react to the impairments, as the human body realistically would).

After the belt, both of my knees were bound with knee wraps to restrict the mobilty in my joint.

Then my elbows were similarly wrapped. In additon, a cervical collar was put on my neck to restrict my head movement.

Next came special gloves worn over the previous set. These were added to simulate restricted hand mobility to futher impair grip ability.

Afterwards, I put on special googles that simulate glaucoma, a variable conditon which generally involves blurry or foggy vision and acute light sensitivity.

In addition to these, wrist weights were added to my arms to simulate sinking strength and unsteady coordination.

The penultimate item was a combination of a weighted vest and a spiked pad beneath the vest. These were added to simulate further simulate postural weakness, mobility restriction, sinking strength, a decreased sense of balance, and restricted bending of my spine.

Finally, a headset was put on me (at this point my mobility and sense of autonomy were severly impaired). These headsets were connected to an mp3 player which played high pitched tones to impair my hearing, simulating tinnitus.

And so the journey began...
