VISUAL IMPAIRMENT APARATUS

SCHOOL OF ART + DESIGN TO GIES SCHOOL OF BUSINESS

This second exercise featured a simulation of two degress of vision impairment, complete and partial vision loss.

This journey begain in the School of Art + Design where I put on a vision impairment apparatus that simulated complete blindness. In addition I was also equipped with a white cane to guide my way.

The objective in this was to simulate the experience of a potential vision impaired user. In this, the goal was to journey to the Gies College of Business unaided.

-
The only forms of guidiance were provided by collegues who supervised my journey and provided words of caution to steer me from any dangers.

The first of several activities of daily living I engaged in along was to drink from the water fountain. During this, I was heavily dependent on my hearing and, unavoidably, my mental model of a water fountain prior to being without sight.

Aftewards, I began navigating my way through the first floor hallway and out of Art + Design. Prior to begining this, i underestimated how heavily I would depend on my hearing.

I listen to every sound, from the tapping of my cane to the footfalls ahead of me, all in an attempt to prevent from running into anything or anyone. I also found myself reflexively adopting a slow rhythmic gait.

Soon I arrived at the north exit in the link gallery. Here was my first major interaction with one of a multitude of architectural design decisions that affect users' experience.

In this, I attempted to find the ADA button to open the automatic doors; finding them proved fairly difficult but I eventually did.

Upon finding and pushing the button, I was able to gain access out of the building.

Once outside, to my surprise, it was actually a lot more difficult to maintain my orientationin the right direction.

Eventually, with the guidance of of my colleagues and adapting to this new condition, I soon fell into a fairly comfortable rhythm.

However, my reflexive anxiety did not subside entirely, and I occasionally had to pause during the journey to reassess and ensure I was indeed on the right path and out of danger.

Eventually, I was able to successfully enter Gies.

Within the building, there were several variable obstacles I had to navigate around, including people, equipment and several others.

Eventually I reached the turning point at Gies, where I gave a reflection about the first half of the journey (see below).

Afterwards, I switched impairment apparatus, put on a device to simulate partial vision loss, and began my journey back...
