EMPATHIC MODELING

Empathic modelling is a research method whereby an individual adopts different tools, wearables or software simulators, to simulate the (dis)abilities of users in everyday scenarios. (Torrens 2011)

Through empathic modeling, the designer aligns with user's felt experience and values, which are critical for defining the basis for the usability and appropriateness of designed artifacts. (Nicolle 2003)

EMPATHIC MODELING FOR
ARCHITECTURAL RESEARCH

This narrative presents accounts of a study, titled "Harnessing Empathy", conducted to assess the viability of empathic modeling as a method for architectural designers to research user experience during the design process. In this, the author embarked on two different journeys to and through three buildings at the University of Illinois while embodying the physical conditions of potential users, in order to understand how design decisions might affect them.
